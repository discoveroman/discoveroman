Essential Apps for Omani Residents and Visitors

  • BankMuscat

    BankMuscat banking on the go is a reality with BankMuscat’s mobile app. As one of Oman’s leading financial institutions, BankMuscat offers its customers a range of services, from checking balances and making payments to transferring money and applying for loans. It’s an essential app for those who want to manage their finances conveniently from their smartphones.

  • Google Maps

    Google Maps is indispensable for anyone traveling in Oman. Whether you’re navigating the roads in Muscat or exploring the countryside, Google Maps provides accurate, real-time navigation with turn-by-turn directions. It also helps you discover nearby restaurants, shops, and tourist attractions. With offline maps, you can even navigate without an internet connection in certain areas.

  • Talabat

    Talabat when hunger strikes, Talabat is the app you need. This popular food delivery service allows you to order meals from a wide range of local and international restaurants. Whether you’re craving pizza, sushi, or traditional Omani dishes, Talabat ensures your food is delivered to your door with just a few taps.

  • Otaxi

    Otaxi is an easy-to-use app for booking taxis in Oman. Whether you’re in Muscat or any other major city in the country, you can book a ride quickly with just a few taps. The app offers real-time tracking, transparent pricing, and multiple payment options. It’s a safe and reliable way to get around Oman's busy streets.

  • WhatsApp

    WhatsApp is one of the most widely used messaging apps in Oman and around the world. It allows you to send instant messages, make voice and video calls, and share multimedia like photos and videos. WhatsApp is an essential app for both personal and business communication, offering secure end-to-end encryption and a wide range of group chat features.

  • OpenSouq

    OpenSouq for those looking to buy or sell products locally, OpenSouq is Oman’s go-to classifieds app. Whether you’re looking for furniture, electronics, cars, or even jobs, this platform connects buyers and sellers, making it easier than ever to find what you need within Oman’s market.

  • The Royal Oman Police

    The Royal Oman Police (ROP) app acts as a digital portal, providing access to a range of essential police services. It streamlines interactions with the ROP by enabling users to check traffic violations and pay fines, renew private vehicle registration licenses, and track visa application statuses. Crucially, it offers rapid access to emergency services via 9999 and helps locate nearby police stations.

  • The Ministry of Health app

    The MOH Oman app, developed by the Ministry of Health, serves as a vital resource for healthcare information and services within the country. It prioritizes the dissemination of up-to-date health information, covering disease prevention, health awareness campaigns, and public health advisories. The app aims to facilitate access to healthcare services by potentially providing information on healthcare facilities such as hospitals and clinics.
